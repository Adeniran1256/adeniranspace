About Me

I am Adeniran Gbadebo Seun, a Doctoral student in Space Geophysics at National Institute for Space Research, Brazil. My research focuses on the dynamics of the ionosphere and understanding the impacts of various space weather events on the ionosphere.

Academic background: MSc in Solid Earth Physics (University of Ibadan, Nigeria), BTech in Pure and Applieed Physics (LAUTECH, Nigeria).

Impact of SSW events on Ionosphere

My research focuses on the effects of Sudden Stratospheric Warming (SSW) on the ionosphere. SSW events lead to significant temperature increases in the stratosphere, which can disrupt atmospheric circulation and influence ionospheric dynamics. This study aims to understand how these events affect ionospheric parameters like electron density and total electron content (TEC). By analyzing observational data, I seek to uncover the implications of SSW on radio wave propagation and satellite communication, enhancing our understanding of space weather phenomena.
